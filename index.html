Projects

The Toolkit Software

How to use AI to automate analysis in eating behavior?


I am developing a software to track how fast people eat, count the number of bites, chews, and sips, and even measure who is the fastest researcher to eat a cookie! All without lifting a finger, thanks to the deployment of computer vision learning models, and software engineering.

Eatpol

Can we count bites, chews, and sips from video?
For this project, I created a dataset from videos of people eating a meal and I trained video vision transformers in Pytorch to predict bites, chews, and sips.

The faster you eat, the more you eat!
This video classification model is useful to calculate how fast people eat and will be used by eating behavior scientists to develop strategies that can decrease the eating rate

Foodpedia in Collaboration with MIT Senseable City Lab

How does the urban food environment impact health outcomes? Does the availability of certain foods in our neighborhoods affect our health? This research paper is currently under review at Nature Foods.
For this project, I employed data mining, SQL databases, and sentence transformers to analyze and quantify the nutritional content of foods across neighborhoods in Boston, London, and Dubai.

Bitecounter

Can we use facial landmarks to count bites automatically?
I created a rule-based program that uses distances between facial landmarks to count bites from video files or in real-time.
Check out the publication and the demo!
